      In the case of a proxy war... generally you have two larger belligerents who arent in direct war with each other supplying or fighting for opposing sides in a civil war or conflict between two other nations.

      • The word "proxy" has it roots in the Latin word "procurare" for "management" or "administration." In medieval times, the term "procurator" or "proctor" came to mean an agent who acted with authority on behalf of another in an official capacity.

        In politics and war, "proxy" often relates to conflicts between parties who are actually acting on the interests of other, usually larger, parties who remain in the background.

        The Cold War period saw numerous examples of proxy conflicts ostensibly between local groups in small countries around the world who were backed by either the USA or Soviet Union.

      • A proxy war is an armed conflict between two states or non-state actors which act on the instigation or on behalf of other parties that are not directly involved in the hostilities. In order for a conflict to be considered a proxy war, there must be a direct, long-term relationship between external actors and the belligerents involved.The aforementioned relationship usually takes the form of funding, military training, arms, or other forms of material assistance which assist a belligerent party in sustaining its war effort.

        Proxy

        noun,
        the agency, function, or power of a person authorized to act as the deputy or substitute for another.
