Maria,
        To me democratization means equal access to resources and P2P means using a commuication medium that lets you to send messages directly to a specific end user. The interor a medi that really LANs(Local area network)/WANs(Wide area network)/subnets place. Your ISP is essentially the one running the local network that your home computer belongs to.
      The real question I believe you're raising is what happens when private groups of citizens or whole towns want to become their own ISP and control their own internet connection infrastructure. If I'm wrong on that score, please let me know. It's an interesting idea to create an ISP as a public utility owned and operated by a municipal government, but I'm not sure how prepared most cities or towns would control for the ongoing costs in both equipment and personnel needed to maintain an ISP data center(s) using per household fees or taxes to pay for it. Private citizens or neighbors can, of course, do whatever they want if it's their own money being used to set up such a local ISP. But it's still expensive and someone has to mind the store 24/7 to make sure connections are maintained to an internet backbone service.
      With a blockchain style database you can take advantage of saving data across several machines on any given network or the internet. However, a blockchain db won't  necessarily add any other value over a standard, secured RDBMS in this scenario.
